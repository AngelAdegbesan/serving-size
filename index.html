Why You Should Read Your Food Labels

Packaged foods and drinks—the types that come in cans, boxes, bottles, jars, and bags—have a lot of nutrition and food safety information on their labels or packaging. Reading them is the first step in making informed decisions about what you consume.

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA made the first major update to the food labels in over 20 years. The label's updated information makes it easier for consumers to be informed on food items that could contribute to healthy eating habits. The updated food label includes mandatory information about “added sugars” and “trans-fat.” Because Americans do not always get enough vitamin D and potassium, the updated food label includes serving information for both of these nutrients.
